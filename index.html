Expressive Arts and Design

Read time: 2 minutes

Expressive arts and design is one of the 'specific' areas of the EYFS framework, encouraging children to build upon their creativity and imagination.

EDI can be implemented into this area in the form of artwork such as painting henna on cutouts, using chalk to creat mandalas, encouraging storytelling and pretend play from different perspectives to develop their empathy, and incorporating cooking into settings to give children a chance to learn more about international cuisines.

Physical Development

Read time: 3 minutes

Physical development is one of the primary areas of development within the EYFS framework, focused on enhancing the gross and fine motor skills of children.

"One of my favourite activities during my time as an Early Years Practitioner was sharing my culture with the children by teaching them Bhangra dancing. It was uplifting seeing their enthusiasm to learn something different to what they are used to." Emma, 2024

EDI can be implemented into this area in the form of teaching children traditional dances or incorporating sports such as karate. Cultural history of sports such as yofa, originating from India, can also educated children in an engaging way. Being inclusive to children of all abilities can be implemented by creating obstacle courses accessible to everyone.

Understanding the World

Read time: 2 minutes

Understanding the world is another 'specific' area of the EYFS framework, helping children to develop a sense of the world and environment around them.

EDI can be implemented into this area by ensuring that the story books within settings are diverse and share perspectives from a variety of different characters, perhaps from different cultures or religions. Settings could also bring in a diverse range og guest speakers to share information about their experiences which gives children an open chance to ask questions and learn more.
